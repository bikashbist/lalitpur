Lumbini Development Trust

The Lumbini Development Trust (LDT) was established in 1985 as an autonomous, non-profit organization under the Lumbini Development Trust Act. The main objective of the Trust is to develop Lumbini as a Buddhist pilgrimage site and a symbol of world peace.

Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Rupandehi district of Nepal. The Trust is responsible for the preservation, restoration, and development of this sacred site, ensuring it remains accessible to pilgrims and visitors from around the world.

Under the guidance of the Ministry of Culture, Tourism and Civil Aviation, the Trust implements the Lumbini Master Plan, which was designed by the renowned Japanese architect Kenzo Tange in 1978. This comprehensive plan divides the Lumbini area into three zones: Sacred Garden, Monastic Zone, and New Lumbini Village.

Our Mission

To develop Lumbini as a world peace city and Buddhist pilgrimage center, preserving its historical and archaeological significance while promoting cultural and religious harmony.

We are committed to implementing the Lumbini Master Plan, conserving the sacred birthplace of Lord Buddha, and creating infrastructure that serves pilgrims and visitors from around the world.

Our Vision

To establish Lumbini as a premier destination for spiritual tourism and a global symbol of peace, fostering understanding and harmony among different cultures and religions.

We envision Lumbini as a vibrant center for Buddhist studies, meditation, and cultural exchange, contributing to the socio-economic development of the region while preserving its sacred heritage.

Our History

1967

UN Secretary-General U Thant visits Lumbini and initiates international interest in developing Lumbini as a Buddhist pilgrimage site.

1970

International Committee for the Development of Lumbini is formed under the leadership of Nepal.

1978

Japanese architect Kenzo Tange completes the Lumbini Master Plan, dividing the area into three zones.

1985

Lumbini Development Trust is established under the Lumbini Development Trust Act to implement the Master Plan.

1997

UNESCO declares Lumbini as a World Heritage Site, recognizing its universal value and historical significance.

2010

Major restoration projects begin in the Sacred Garden area, including the Mayadevi Temple.

2025

Celebration of the 2569th Buddha Jayanti, marking significant progress in the development of Lumbini as a world peace city.
